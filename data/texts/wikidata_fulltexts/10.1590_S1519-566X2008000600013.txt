(Lepidoptera: Gelechiidae)
ABSTRACT - Dichloromethane (DIC) leaf and fruit extracts of Trichiliapallida Swartz were 
obtained for the isolation and identifi cation of molecules with insecticidal activity against the tomato 
leafminer, Tuta absoluta (Meyrick). DIC leaf extracts of T. pallida yielded six compounds, the 
triterpenes 24-methylenecycloarta-3E-ol (TRIT-1), 24-methylenecycloarta-3E-26-diol (TRIT-2) and 
cycloarta-23-eno-3E,25-diol (TRIT-3), the sterols 24-methylene-3,22-dihydroxycholesterol (EST-1), 
24-methylenecholesterol (EST-2) and 24-methylene-3E,4E,22-trihydroxycholesterol (EST-3), while 
the fruit extract yielded the limonoid gedunine (LIM). These molecules were dissolved in acetone 
and sprayed at 0.1% on tomato leafl ets infested with newly-hatched larvae. Larval mortality at day 
5 and 9 after infestation, larval and pupal developmental time and survival, pupal weight and adult 
malformation were evaluated. TRIT-1, EST-1 and LIM were the most effective against T. absoluta
due to larval development arrestment and reduced larval survivorship.
